Every environment that ships with NeMo Gym lives in [`resources_servers/`](https://github.com/NVIDIA-NeMo/Gym/tree/main/resources_servers) and has a config under `resources_servers/<name>/configs/`. Filter by domain, scorer pattern, or verified status — each card links to the server's source on GitHub.

## Status Legend

The `verified:` field in each YAML config tracks whether the team has baselined and reviewed the benchmark.

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Verified** | `verified: true` — baselined, expected output documented, reward profile reviewed. Safe to use as a published-number reference. |
| **Unverified** | `verified: false` — runnable, but expected output is not locked. Fine for development; cross-check before publishing numbers. |

Most shipped benchmarks today are `verified: false`. That's not a quality signal — it tracks formal baselining, not whether the benchmark works. The team is actively raising coverage; new entries start unverified and graduate as baselines are locked.

## How to Add a Benchmark to This Catalog

The pre-commit hook `update-readme-table` auto-adds new YAML configs to the root `README.md` table. This catalog is data-driven from `fern/components/benchmarks/benchmarks-data.ts` — when you ship a new resources server, append an entry there with its domain and scorer pattern.

For the full author flow — resources server, verifier, dataset, baselining — refer to:

- [Add a Benchmark](/latest/build-environments/integrations/add-a-benchmark) — full author tutorial.

A new benchmark earns the `verified: true` flag once it has a baselined expected output, a documented validation recipe (refer to [Validate Your Setup](/latest/evaluate/run/validate-your-setup)), and team review.
